NALDA

At the National Agricultural Land Development Authority, we are saddled with the mandate of harnessing the full potentials of the vast arable lands in Nigeria, empowering small Holder and large Scale Farmers and mechanize, support the drive for Food and Fiber security while assisting in diversifying the nation's economy, improving household incomes and enhancing revenue mobilization and generation nationwide.

About Us

We intend to carry out our mandate entrusted to us by the Presidency by initiating and carrying out different programes and initiatives, like the following:

Land Survey / Acquisition and Allocation

In our quest to harness the vast potentials of the Nigerian Land, we are tasked with surveying, acquiring and distributing lands to farmers, the more farmers we have cultivating, the higher our economic output as a nation.

Subsidy & Grant Distribution

For new and old farmers, we are required to provide assistance in allocating subsidies and grants to farmers, we are mandated to give intellectual assistance, and subsidies to mechanize farming operations in the Nigerian farm lands.

Youth and Women Empowerment

We believe that the future belong to the youth, and the importance of making agriculture attractive to the citizens of our country, especially the youth is not lost on us, we believe in catching them young, and in that spirit we organize seminars and workshops to teach new and innovative farming techniques.

Our Programmes

In accordance with our mandate, we have set up some programmes to hit the ground running in fulfilling our mandate

Buhari Young Farmers Network

Under this programme, NALDA will focus on encouraging the Nigerian youth to participate in agriculture. This projects final goal would be to encourage, empower and guide 1000 farmers from the 774 LGAs in Nigeria. This programme is intended to cut accross all facets of agriculture.

Volunteer Programme

Under the volunteer, we are calling on the Nigerian youth to volunteer their massive and impressive talents in establishing and running the full NALDA networks accross the nation, we intend to engage the youth with this directive, and those chosen will be the pioneers conquering the frontiers of Job creation and stability in Agricultural Sector. This programme intends to engage 5 graduate volunteers in each of the 36 states of the country and the FCT.

Back to Farm Initiative

Under this initiative, we intend to drum up awareness in the populace, highlighting the importance of going back to the farm, as farming the NALDA way has been made attractive by incorporating new and innovative methods of farming. We will be in cahoots with various paramilitary outfits in the country, along with our NYSC, and various professionals and associations.

Agro Input Intervention

This intervention will be geared towards active farmers to help them expand, we will provide subsidies in collaboration with the Agricultural Development Project, Value Chain Actors, State Ministries of Agriclture and Co-operative bodies.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is NALDA?
NALDA (National Agricultural Land Development Authority), is a body established in 1992 but it went into extinction in 2000. However under the administration of the President Muhammadu Buhari, it was revived to carry out its mandate of developing rural communities through agriculture and to make agriculture a business and a source of wealth creation for the country.
How does NALDA help the society?
NALDA intends to provide food security for the populace of the country, and inadvertently provide Jobs and meaningful engagements for the youth. We also intend to reduce the bar of entering agriculture as a farmer by providing subsidies and grants to increase output.
What Programmes are currently running under the NALDA initiative?
The following programmes are currently running under the NALDA initiative:
  • Buhari Young Farmers Network
  • Volunteer Programme
  • Back to Farm Initiative
  • Agro Input Initiative
What is the Buhari Young Farmers Network?
Under the Buhari Young Farmers Network, we intend to inject 774,000 new farmers into the agricuyltural sector. We are currnetly running a pilot programme that involves 100 farmers from easch of the 774 LGAs of the country.
What is the Volunteer Programme?
Under the Volunteer Programme, we intend to engage 5 graduate volunteers from each state of country including the FCT, to be pioneers in establishing the NALDA network and help interface with the farmers to carry out our mandate.
What is the Back to Farm Initiative?
The Back to Farm initiative is a programme designed to raise public awareness about the NALDA initiative and its mandate to make agriculture attractive to the youth, and engage farmers across all facets of agriculture.
